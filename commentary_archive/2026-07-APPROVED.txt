Vulnerability has climbed further to 0.91 this month, driven by a CAPE near 41, credit-spread compression that has tightened to an extreme 0.48% Baa–Aaa differential, and margin-loan leverage that has expanded 28% year-over-year — the system carries more accumulated fragility than it did last month, and the valuation and credit gauges are now operating near the outer edges of their historical ranges. The trigger reading has fallen to 0.22, and none of the ignition gauges — spread widening, rising volatility, or price momentum deterioration — show active stress at this time; the state therefore remains Elevated rather than Critical, and that distinction matters. The combination of extreme vulnerability alongside a quiet trigger is the defining feature of this reading: conditions are unusually combustible, but no spark is currently visible in the data.

The qualitative risk the framework is least equipped to capture is a sudden, disorderly repricing of collateral quality in AI-linked credit — specifically, the possibility that a cluster of data-center or infrastructure borrowers faces covenant stress or a ratings action simultaneously, at a moment when the secondary market for that paper is thin and dealer balance-sheet capacity to absorb selling is limited; because this credit is spread across leveraged-loan, investment-grade, and private-credit structures, a localized disruption could transmit more broadly before spread indices fully reflect it. The widening gap between a near-ceiling vulnerability score and a still-subdued trigger is worth monitoring precisely because the historical record suggests that when the system is this primed, the move from quiet to stressed can be faster than spread momentum gauges register in real time.
