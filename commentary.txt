Vulnerability remains deeply elevated at 0.82, with valuation, credit-spread compression, and margin-loan leverage all near the upper end of their historical ranges — the system continues to carry substantial dry tinder. That said, the trigger reading has edged down to 0.28 and none of the ignition gauges — spread widening, volatility, price momentum, or conditions tightening — are flashing active stress, so the state remains elevated rather than critical. The modest softening in trigger components relative to last month is a small note of stability, but it does not reduce the underlying fragility that the vulnerability gauges describe. The qualitative risk the framework cannot fully quantify is a sudden repricing of AI-linked capital expenditure expectations: corporate debt issuance financing data-center buildout has grown large enough that a credible signal of delayed monetization — whether from an earnings miss, a large customer pulling back on infrastructure commitments, or a regulatory constraint on model deployment — could simultaneously derate the concentrated index leaders and widen credit spreads in the names that have borrowed to fund that build-out, a combination that would move both vulnerability and trigger gauges quickly. Geopolitical disruption to semiconductor supply chains remains a related tail, given how much of the AI investment thesis depends on uninterrupted access to advanced chips. Nothing in the current data suggests that ignition is underway; the note is one of sustained watchfulness, not alarm.
